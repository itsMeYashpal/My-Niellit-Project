UNICEF

Working in the U.S. to Help the World's Children

We collaborate with governments and corporations, non-profit groups and social enterprises, civic leaders and celebrities, churches and other faith-based organizations, youth groups and campus groups, teachers and students and families — anyone willing to help us advocate for the survival and well-being of every child.

Helping every child thrive, all over the world

Over eight decades, the United Nations Children’s Fund (UNICEF) has built an unprecedented global support system for the world’s children. UNICEF relentlessly works day in and day out to deliver the essentials that give every child an equitable chance in life: health care and immunizations, safe water and sanitation, nutrition, education, emergency relief and more. UNICEF USA advances the global mission of UNICEF by rallying the American public to support the world’s most vulnerable children. Together, we have helped save more children’s lives than any other humanitarian organization.

Where UNICEF Works

UNICEF works in more than 190 countries and territories all over the world to save and protect vulnerable children and families. Here are some of the places where UNICEF has been focusing its work recently:

  • Bangladesh
  • Central African Republic
  • Central America
  • Haiti
  • Iraq
  • Nigeria
  • South Sudan
  • Syria
  • Yemen

What UNICEF Is Doing

UNICEF works to ensure that children's basic needs are met, to protect their rights and address major threats to their health, safety and well being. Areas of focus include:

  • COVID-19
  • Ebola
  • HIV/AIDS
  • Immunization
  • Nutrition
  • Water

Emergency Relief

UNICEF responds to hundreds of emergencies every year, focusing on:

  • saving and protecting children in conflict
  • protecting the rights of child refugees and migrants
  • delivering lifesaving assistance to children and families affected by earthquakes, floods, hurricanes, tsunamis and other natural disasters
